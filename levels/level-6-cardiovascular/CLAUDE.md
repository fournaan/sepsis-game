# CLAUDE.md — Level 6: Cardiovascular System

## Setting
Inside the bloodstream. Reduced gravity throughout. Oxygen levels and pressure vary by vessel.
Route: peripheral left arm artery → fistula → brachial vein → subclavian → SVC → heart.

## Unique Mechanic
Gravity reduction in the bloodstream. Pressure and flow speed change per vessel segment.
Final sub-level is inside the heart — valve platforms used as jump boosters in the boss fight.

## Enemies
- **Henchmen:** E. coli, MRSA, Pseudomonas
- **Boss (3-phase):** Strep viridans → Staph aureus → Kingella (Infective Endocarditis)

## Obstacles
| Obstacle | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Cholesterol plaques | Dodge floating lipid deposits |
| Pressors | Vasoconstiction / vasodilation alters corridor width |
| Rapid AF | Irregular, arrhythmic floor movement |
| Septic shower / cytokine storm | Screen-wide damage burst |
| Venepuncture needle | Avoid in brachial vein segment |
| Lymphatic dump | Special hazard at left subclavian junction |
| Valves | Jump boosters in final boss arena |

## Special Weapons (heart sub-level only)
- **Adrenaline** — counters asystole obstacle
- **DCCV (DC cardioversion)** — counters VT obstacle

## Boss Fight
Three sequential phases inside the heart, one per IE organism.
Infected valve is located by navigating through a PFO (patent foramen ovale).
Valves double as jump platforms during combat.

## Antibiotic Unlocks
- Benzylpenicillin (Strep viridans IE — phase 1 reward)
- Vancomycin + Gentamicin (Staph A IE — phase 2 reward)
- Amoxicillin (Kingella IE — phase 3 reward)

## Hints
- Sepsis 6 (the key educational goal of this level)
- Septic screen: CXR, urine MCS, blood cultures, TOE
- IE organisms and which valve they prefer
- Murmurs — which valve is infected
- Arrhythmias and when to shock
- Vasopressors in septic shock

## Win Condition
Defeat all 3 IE bosses → patient is cured. End screen.
